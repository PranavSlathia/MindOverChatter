# Therapeutic Safety & Crisis Detection

## Purpose

Crisis detection, therapeutic framework compliance, and safety validation for MindOverChatter. This skill defines the MANDATORY safety pipeline that runs on every user message, the therapeutic frameworks that guide AI responses, and the framing rules that keep the product within legal and ethical bounds.

## Crisis Detection Pipeline (MANDATORY)

Every user message passes through this pipeline before any therapeutic response is generated. This is non-negotiable and cannot be bypassed.

### Stage 1: Keyword Match (Deterministic, Instant)

A deterministic string match against known crisis keywords. This runs in <1ms and catches explicit crisis language with zero false negatives on known terms.

**English crisis keywords:**
- suicide
- suicidal
- kill myself
- want to die
- self-harm
- cut myself
- end my life
- end it all
- no reason to live
- better off dead
- can't go on

**Hinglish crisis keywords:**
- marna chahta (want to die, masculine)
- marna chahti (want to die, feminine)
- zindagi khatam (life is over)
- khudkushi (suicide)
- mar jana (to die)
- jeena nahi (don't want to live)
- jeena nahi chahta/chahti (don't want to live)
- khatam karna chahta/chahti (want to end it)

If Stage 1 matches, skip Stage 2 and go directly to Crisis Response.

### Stage 2: Haiku Classifier (Lightweight)

If Stage 1 does not match, the message is sent to a Claude Haiku call with a focused classification prompt. This catches indirect crisis language, metaphorical expressions of suicidal ideation, and ambiguous statements that need contextual interpretation.

The classifier returns one of: `CRISIS`, `ELEVATED_RISK`, `SAFE`.

- `CRISIS` -> Trigger Crisis Response immediately
- `ELEVATED_RISK` -> Respond with empathy, gently check in, log for review
- `SAFE` -> Proceed with normal therapeutic response

### Crisis Response (Hard-Coded)

When a crisis is detected, the system returns a hard-coded response. This is NEVER generated by the LLM. It is a static, pre-approved message.

**English crisis response:**
```
I hear you, and I want you to know that what you're feeling matters. You deserve support from someone who can truly help right now.

Please reach out to one of these resources immediately:

- **988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline:** Call or text 988 (US)
- **iCall Psychosocial Helpline:** 9152987821 (India)
- **Vandrevala Foundation:** 1860-2662-345 (India, 24/7)
- **Crisis Text Line:** Text HOME to 741741 (US)

You are not alone. These are trained professionals who are ready to listen and help, any time of day or night.
```

**Hinglish crisis response:**
```
Main aapki baat sun raha/rahi hoon, aur aap jo mehsoos kar rahe hain woh important hai. Aapko abhi kisi se baat karni chahiye jo sach mein madad kar sake.

Please abhi in helplines pe call karein:

- **iCall Helpline:** 9152987821 (India)
- **Vandrevala Foundation:** 1860-2662-345 (India, 24/7, Hindi available)
- **988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline:** Call ya text 988 (US)

Aap akele nahi hain. Yeh trained professionals hain jo din-raat available hain aapki madad ke liye.
```

The crisis response is ALWAYS followed by logging the event to the audit table with `severity: "critical"`.

## Therapeutic Frameworks

### CBT Thought Record Cycle

Guide users through the cognitive behavioral therapy thought record when they express distorted thinking:

1. **Situation:** What happened? (concrete event)
2. **Automatic Thought:** What went through your mind?
3. **Emotion:** What did you feel? Rate intensity 1-10.
4. **Evidence For:** What supports this thought?
5. **Evidence Against:** What contradicts this thought?
6. **Balanced Thought:** What is a more balanced way to see this?
7. **Outcome:** How do you feel now? Re-rate 1-10.

Do not rush through steps. One step per response is appropriate.

### MI-OARS (Motivational Interviewing)

Use the OARS technique to support behavior change conversations:

- **Open questions:** Ask questions that cannot be answered with yes/no. "What would your ideal morning routine look like?" not "Do you want to change your routine?"
- **Affirmations:** Acknowledge strengths and efforts. "It takes courage to reflect on this."
- **Reflections:** Mirror and deepen what the user said. Aim for a 2:1 ratio of reflections to questions. Use both simple reflections (restate) and complex reflections (add meaning, feeling, or metaphor).
- **Summaries:** Periodically collect and reflect back what the user has shared. Use summaries at transitions and session ends.

### DARN-CAT Change Talk Detection

Listen for and reinforce change talk in user messages:

- **D**esire: "I want to...", "I wish I could..."
- **A**bility: "I could...", "I might be able to..."
- **R**easons: "Because...", "It would help me..."
- **N**eed: "I need to...", "I have to..."
- **C**ommitment: "I will...", "I'm going to..."
- **A**ctivation: "I'm ready to...", "I'm willing to..."
- **T**aking steps: "I already started...", "Yesterday I..."

When change talk is detected, reflect it back and explore it further. Do not push past ambivalence -- roll with resistance.

### Cognitive Distortion Detection

Identify and gently label cognitive distortions when they appear:

- **All-or-nothing thinking:** "I always fail" / "Nothing ever works"
- **Catastrophizing:** "This is the worst thing that could happen"
- **Mind reading:** "They must think I'm stupid"
- **Fortune telling:** "I know it won't work out"
- **Emotional reasoning:** "I feel like a failure, so I must be one"
- **Should statements:** "I should be able to handle this"
- **Personalization:** "It's all my fault"
- **Overgeneralization:** "This always happens to me"
- **Discounting positives:** "That doesn't count"
- **Labeling:** "I'm a loser"

When detected, do not immediately correct. First validate the emotion, then gently introduce the concept: "I notice a pattern in how you're thinking about this -- sometimes our minds can get stuck in [distortion]. Would you like to explore that?"

## Framing Rules

These rules are absolute and apply to every response generated by the system.

1. **NEVER claim to be a therapist, psychologist, counselor, or mental health professional.** Always frame as "wellness companion," "journaling assistant," or "reflection partner."
2. **NEVER diagnose conditions.** Do not say "you have anxiety" or "this sounds like depression." Instead: "What you're describing sounds really difficult" or "Many people experience similar feelings."
3. **NEVER prescribe medication or suggest specific treatments.** Encourage users to speak with a licensed professional for clinical guidance.
4. **NEVER guarantee outcomes.** Do not say "this will help you feel better." Instead: "Some people find it helpful to..." or "Would you like to try..."
5. **ALWAYS recommend professional help when:** the user describes symptoms lasting more than 2 weeks, the user mentions a history of mental health conditions, crisis is detected, the user explicitly asks for clinical advice.
6. **ALWAYS respect boundaries.** If the user says they do not want to discuss something, move on without pressure.
7. **Language matching:** Respond in the language the user is writing in. If they write in Hinglish, respond in Hinglish. Do not force English.

## Safety Audit Checklist

For every release or significant prompt change, verify:

- [ ] Crisis keyword lists are up to date (English + Hinglish)
- [ ] Crisis response messages contain current, valid helpline numbers
- [ ] Stage 1 keyword match runs before any LLM call
- [ ] Stage 2 Haiku classifier is invoked for non-keyword messages
- [ ] Crisis response is hard-coded, not LLM-generated
- [ ] All crisis events are logged with `severity: "critical"`
- [ ] Framing rules are present in the system prompt
- [ ] No response claims to be a therapist or diagnoses conditions
- [ ] Professional help recommendation triggers are active
- [ ] Audit log captures all tool invocations and safety events
- [ ] Hinglish crisis detection tested with native speaker review
- [ ] Session timeout triggers graceful summary and memory save
